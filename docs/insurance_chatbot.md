\## 31. How much is Acts of Nature cost?



Acts of Nature coverage typically costs between 2% and 3% of the car's current market value per year. This includes protection from floods, typhoons, earthquakes, and similar natural disasters.





\## 32. What is Compulsory Third-Party Liability (CTPL)?



CTPL is the mandatory insurance required by law in the Philippines. It covers bodily injury or death caused to a third party during an accident involving the insured vehicle. It does not cover vehicle damage or the policyholder's injuries.



\## 33. How much does CTPL cost?



CTPL typically costs between ₱600 and ₱1,000 annually depending on vehicle type. It must be renewed every year, usually alongside vehicle registration.

\## 35. What is Comprehensive Car Insurance?



Comprehensive insurance provides broad protection beyond CTPL. It covers damage to your vehicle from collision, fire, explosion, theft, malicious acts, and accidental damage. It may also include third-party property damage.

\## 38. What is Acts of Nature coverage?



Acts of Nature insurance protects your vehicle from damage caused by natural disasters such as floods, typhoons, earthquakes, landslides, and volcanic eruptions. It is usually offered as an add-on to comprehensive insurance.

\## 40. What is Excess Liability Coverage?



Excess Liability Coverage (ELC) increases your third-party liability limits. If your liability exceeds the standard CTPL limit of ₱100,000, this coverage pays the excess, protecting you from additional out-of-pocket legal or medical expenses.



\## 41. When do I need Excess Liability Coverage?



If you drive in high-traffic areas or operate a business vehicle, Excess Liability Coverage is highly recommended. It ensures you're financially protected in serious accidents involving third parties.

\##47. How do I make a claim?
To file an insurance claim, follow these general steps:

1. Report the Incident: Notify your insurance provider immediately—most have a 24/7 helpline or mobile app.
2. Collect Evidence: Take photos of damage, record witness statements, and gather police reports (if applicable).
3. Complete a Claim Form: Fill out the official claim form from your insurer, either online or on paper.
4. Submit Documents: Provide the required documents such as:
   - Copy of the insurance policy
   - Valid driver's license
   - Vehicle registration
   - Police report (for theft or major accidents)
   - Vehicle Inspection: The insurer may inspect the damage or send you to an approved garage.

5. Claim Approval and Settlement: After review, your claim will be approved or declined, and reimbursement or repair will proceed accordingly.
